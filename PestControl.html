- Common Garden Pests and How to Control Them -


- In gardens, pests such as aphids, caterpillars, slugs, and beetles can damage plants. Identifying pests early is crucial to preventing widespread damage.

- Pests are most active during the growing season, so it's important to monitor your garden regularly for signs of infestation.

- To keep pests away, try natural pest control methods such as introducing beneficial insects, using organic sprays, and maintaining a healthy garden environment to reduce pest habitat.



1: Identifying Common Garden Pests

1. The first step in pest control is identifying the pests in your garden. Look for common pests like aphids, which are small insects that feed on plant sap.

2. Check the undersides of leaves and new growth for tiny, soft-bodied pests.

3. To manage aphids, you can spray the affected areas with a mixture of water and mild soap to dislodge the pests.



2: Preventing Slug Infestation

1. Slugs can be a major problem for your garden, especially after rain. They tend to feed on tender leaves and vegetables.

2. Place copper tape around the perimeter of your garden or plants to deter slugs, as they dislike copper.

3. Use beer traps to lure and eliminate slugs naturally.



3: Encourage healthy soil.

Healthy soil makes healthy plants with strong immune systems, which are better able to fight off diseases and pests. Healthy soils feed and shelter beneficial soil life.

Natural fertilizers help build healthy soil. Fish and seaweed fertilizer, used once a month, can activate soil microbes.



4: Rotate crops

Crop rotation confuses pests, reduces their concentration in specific areas, and helps you manage soil fertility.
Leave two to three years between planting members of the same crop family in a particular area.

5: Choose resistant varieties

This is an easy tip for preventing garden pests: Choose plant varieties that are naturally resistant to pests. Seed catalogs list varieties that are known for resistance.
For example, tromboncino squash appears to be more resistant to pests than other summer squash varieties.
